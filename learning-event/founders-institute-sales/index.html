The Core

The 4-step process. Same every time.

Run this in order. Skip a step — you don't close. Most founders skip 1, 2, 3 and go straight to 4. That's why nothing converts.

1

Understand your prospect

Listen first. Talk second. Open-ended questions. Find out what's actually true — not what they politely say.

If you're talking, you're losing.

📖 Deep dive on Substack
2

Make sure it's worth their time listening to you

Why should they listen? Establish credibility before you say one word about your product.

No earned attention = no permission to pitch.

3

Do we see the world through the same lens?

Show them a worldview, not a feature list. Do they agree with your read of their problem?

Shared lens = bridge to solution.

4

An actual solution to their problem

Only now you talk product, price, mechanism. By this point they're leaning in — not being pitched at.

Solution is the easy part.

⚠ The founder failure mode

Most founders skip 1, 2, 3 and go straight to 4. People don't care about your solution. They care about their own problems.

Section 3 · Things to Unlearn

What to stop doing in your next validation call.

1

Understand. Understand. Understand the problem.

Forget pitching for a second. Spend your first call learning their problem better than they can describe it themselves. Diagnose, don't pitch. Their language about the problem becomes your script.

2

When you want something, be open about it.

Pretending it's not an ask is worse than asking. Say what you need — intro, feedback, a paid pilot. Lean into the discomfort. Every. Single. Time. The discomfort is the job.

3

Never leave without a next step.

Sales isn't binary. Not every meeting is a close — but every meeting is a next commitment. The next call, the next intro, the next decision. Drive who drives next.

💰 Price

Set one today. Get rejected. That's how you learn what they actually pay for. Interviews validate problems. Rejection validates price.

Five principles. Three books.

Give more than you take.
2
Always be useful.
3
Always help.
4
Obsess over the customer problem — not your solution.
5
Speak about them before you speak about you.
