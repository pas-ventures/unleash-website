Dabchick Wilderness Reserve · South Africa

WHERE
IT BEGAN

A rhino calf, born just before we arrived. Heavy rain during the birth — and an infected ear. During morning treatment, the calf spooked and disappeared into the bush. Tiny. Sick. Untagged.

02
Three Days Of Drone Searches

THE SEARCH

Basil and his team — up day and night. Hyenas in the bush. A reserve too big to walk. On day three, the calf was found and reunited with her mother. The relief lasted minutes — the ear was still bad. The vet was called.
03
The Vet Is En Route

THE
CALL

A helicopter inbound — Robinson R44, fuel cans on the strip. Every hand on standby. Phil and Kira pulled into the operation instead of left waiting on the radio.

04
Two Metres From The Mother

HANDS ON
THE GROUND

Multiple tons darted. Every shoulder needed. The infection had already stopped — the ear came off, and the calf would live.

05
Three Years On

KIRA — THEN & NOW

From a calf hidden in the bush, to a healthy rhino in the green hills of Dabchick. The infection stopped. The ear off. The herd whole.
